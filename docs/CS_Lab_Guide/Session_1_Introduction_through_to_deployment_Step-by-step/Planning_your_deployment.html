Planning your Infinity deployment

More information

Everything you have completed in the Level 1 path to this point has been about giving you the foundational knowledge that we believe is required to architect, administer, and support a Pexip Infinity solution. Now it's time to put this information together and build your deployment. However, this is still a lab environment. Many of the pre-requisites have been completed for you, such as adding the correct DNS records (both internally and externally), setting correct firewall rules and configurations, and even providing certain TLS certificates. Once you have mastered this lab, we encourage you to look deeper and build out your labs in your environments to appreciate what is required fully.

You may be thinking, "well, all this pre-requisite planning and implementation is taken care of by other teams", correct? However, we firmly believe that as a Pexip Infinity architect, administrator, or support personnel, your job involves understanding all these items so that you can communicate effectively with these teams and, importantly, understand when things are not as they should be. Furthermore, much of this information is generic and is used across many kinds of IT infrastructure and understanding these concepts will put you in a much better place.

Let's recap some of the significant points covered in the Level 1 training so far and think about what would be required to build out your lab or customers' environments from scratch.

Virtualisation and Server Design

  1. Ensure you understand how Pexip VM nodes are deployed and map to the underlying physical hardware.
  2. Take your time designing the virtual infrastructure and specifying servers in your customers' environments. This is one of the most critical steps in an Infinity deployment. If you get this wrong, the rest of the system to not function as you might hope.  
    NOTE: We have designed this LAB environment to aid your learning and understanding of Pexip Infinity, and that it can be delivered to hundreds of trainees. As such, there are restrictions regarding the available resources. Please refer to our documentation when designing your systems rather than look at the design of these labs.

DNS Records

  1. DNS records have already been added for you, and exist internally (to the Windows Active Directory domain) and externally in public DNS.
  2. Your lab uses an internal domain space of "pexipacademy.local". Each internal node has a hostname (e.g. "mgr", "conf01", and "conf02"), and we can prepend these to the internal domain to give the nodes Fully Qualified Domain Names (FQDNs), for example "mgr.pexipacademy.local" or "conf01.pexipacademy.local". We can then use these FQDNs to browse to the nodes (e.g., https://mgr.pexipacademy.local or https://conf01.pexipacademy.local), using the Jumpbox sessions.
  3. Your external domain space will be automatically generated for you when you environment is created to avoid overlapping domains with other user's labs. It should look something like "big-cat.pexipacademy.cloud". The external node uses a hostname "edge". You will be able to make external calls into your deployment either by browsing to the edge node using its external FQDN (e.g. https://edge.big-cat.pexipacademey.cloud), or a URI such as sip:vmr@big-cat.pexipacademey.cloud.
  4. You can discover the external DNS records for your lab deployment by using our DNS checker tool: https://dns.pexip.com/.

IP Networking

  1. Your nodes in the lab have a mixture of single and dual NICs, plus static NAT addresses. These interfaces exist in different virtual networks and subnets. Routing has been preconfigured.
  2. It would be best if you had an excellent grasp of IP networking, such as VLANs, IP addressing, subnetting and routing. This forms the core of any modern IP infrastructure project.
  3. You should also ensure you have an excellent grasp of networking theory and the TCP/IP suite of protocols.

TLS Certificates

  1. You will create a TLS certificate that can be applied to the internal nodes using Active Directory Certificate Services (ADCS).
  2. The TLS certificate for the external node has been generated for you and will be referenced from the lab sheet.
  3. It would help understand TLS certificates and Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), how TLS certificates are generated, and how the "chain of trust" works.
